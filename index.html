Creative Journey

How the work evolved from rough sketchbook experiments to structured, client-ready visual systems.

Phase 1 — Foundations in Drawing

Early exploration

Years of studying faces, gesture and light through pencils, pens and traditional tools. No shortcuts — just repetition, anatomy studies and filling sketchbooks to build control over line, value and proportion.

Phase 2 — From Sketches to Clients

Transition to commissioned work

Portrait requests, fan-art commissions and custom pieces for friends, creators and small brands. This phase was all about learning to work with briefs, deadlines and feedback while keeping the style honest and expressive.

Phase 3 — Digital Canvas & Systems

Current focus

Converting handmade art into high-resolution wallpapers, cover art and visual identities. Blending analogue texture with digital polish to design pieces that work across screens, social content, and long-term personal branding.
